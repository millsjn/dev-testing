# Introduction

Welcome to the Coleridge's Administrative Data Research Facility, or "**ADRF**" user guide. This is a living document intended to show new ADRF users how to use the platform for common tasks.

If you have any questions, please reach out to us at [support@coleridgeinitiative.org](mailto:support@coleridgeinitiative.org) 

> [!NOTE]
> All activity within the ADRF is monitored and must follow organizational policies and federal security standards.

## About the Coleridge Initiative
The Coleridge Initiative’s mission is to improve the access, usability, and impact of data for public policy by fostering collaboration between government agencies, researchers, and other stakeholders. It aims to create a data ecosystem where government data can be more effectively used to inform evidence-based decision-making, policy development, and research.

## About the Administrative Data Research Facility
The Administrative Data Research Facility (ADRF) is a platform provided by the Coleridge Initiative that enables researchers to access and analyze administrative data from government agencies securely and efficiently. It offers a secure computing environment where researchers can work with sensitive data while ensuring privacy and confidentiality. The ADRF provides tools and infrastructure for data integration, analysis, and visualization, allowing researchers to conduct rigorous and reproducible studies using large-scale administrative datasets. By facilitating access to valuable data resources and promoting collaboration between researchers, government agencies, and other stakeholders, the ADRF supports evidence-based policymaking and advances research in fields such as public health, education, labor economics, and social policy.

## The Five Safes Security Framework
The ADRF follows the Five Safes Framework, a data management framework commonly used in government agencies, to ensure the safe use of sensitive and confidential data assets. It considers five dimensions in making data-related safety and security decisions: Safe Projects, Safe People, Safe Settings, Safe Data, and Safe Exports. See [Glossary: Five Safe Security Framework](/docs/glossary.md#the-five-safes-security-framework) for more information.

## Mandatory Rules for Avoiding Violations
Users must adhere to all data sharing and non-disclosure agreements.
- **Credential Security**: Do not share authentication credentials, store your password, or use automated logins.
- **Session Security**: You must log out if you leave your computer unattended while logged into the system.
- **Location Security**: Access the ADRF only from a secure location, such as an enclosed, dedicated work environment or a secure office location. You are strictly prohibited from accessing the ADRF from public WiFi or outside the country.
- **Appropriate Use**:
  - Take all notes and perform all writing that involves content hosted in the ADRF within the ADRF.
  - Only share those notes with other approved users on your project.

## Data Confidentiality (Disclosure Rules)
Do not share any data or information that has not been through a formal disclosure process.
- **Prohibited Actions**: You must not take a screen shot of any data point, table, graph, or metadata.
- **Prohibited Notes**: Do not write down any results or take any other form of notes on content hosted in the ADRF outside of the ADRF.
- **Prohibited Sharing**: Do not share or show any information or data within the ADRF to any individual who has not been approved to access the data by the Coleridge Initiative and the Data Steward.

## Removing Output from the ADRF (Disclosure Process)
Nothing can be exported from the ADRF without undergoing a disclosure review that follows protocols approved by the Data Owner.
- **Review Output**: Before submission, review your output to ensure it protects confidentiality and adheres to the data's export rules (e.g., aggregating output to ensure at least 10 individual entities are in a cell).
- **Submit Request**: Submit your export request through the ADRF’s Export Request Module, which is accessed via the ADRF Getting Started page when you log into your project workspace.
- **Review Time**: Account for the disclosure review period, which can take several days.

## Consequences of Violations
Failure to follow these rules will result in **immediate termination of data access**. You may also be prevented from accessing data in the future and may be subject to legal penalties associated with the underlying data use agreements.

## Security Incidents and Support
- **Security Incident Reporting**: If you have any suspicions regarding a security incident or a potential security incident, immediately send an email to `adrf-security@coleridgeinitiative.org` and `support@coleridgeinitiative.org`.
- **General Questions**: For all other questions, contact `support@coleridgeinitiative.org`.
